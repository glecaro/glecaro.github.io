# Gabriela Lecaro Calle

I am a PhD Candidate in Economics at the University of Michigan.  
My research interests lie at the intersection of applied microeconomics, labor, and development economics, with a focus on crime, entrepreneurship, and financial inclusion.

Previously, I was a Research Fellow at the Center for International Development at the Harvard Kennedy School, and a Research Associate at Innovations for Poverty Action.

</section>

<section id="research">

## Research in Progress

### The Price of Safety: The Effects of Extortion on Small-scale Entrepreneurship in Peru  
**with Gustavo Acosta (University of Michigan)**  

I study how extortion affects small-scale entrepreneurs in Peru, focusing on firm survival and investment decisions among convenience stores and microenterprises operating in high-crime environments.

---

### The Effects of Formalization on Gig Worker Labor Supply  
**with Laura Kurczyn (University of Michigan)**  

This project examines how formalization and social protection shape gig workers’ labor supply decisions, with an emphasis on heterogeneity across workers and institutional context.
